Introduction

In the United States, the issue of mass incarceration has been a long-standing and complex problem, disproportionately affecting people of color, particularly Black Americans. The racial disparities in incarceration rates are rooted in a combination of historical and contemporary factors, including systemic racism, discriminatory policies, and socioeconomic inequalities. To better understand and address this pressing issue, it is crucial to analyze incarceration trends and patterns across different demographic groups and geographic regions. For this analysis, I have chosen to focus on the total_jail_pop variable, which represents the yearly jail population for each county or state. This variable is particularly relevant as it provides insights into the number of individuals detained in local jails, which often serve as entry points into the broader criminal justice system. By examining the jail population data, we can shed light on potential disparities and inequalities at the local level, which can inform policy decisions and reform efforts.
